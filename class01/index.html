    • ethical considerations
    • bibliography

Settings

  • Academic

Research Questions

  • Basic vs. Applied
  • Broad vs. Narrow
  • Substantive vs. Methodological
  • What, Why, How
  • Feasibility
  • Connection to literature
  • Connection to research agenda
 

Examples

    Do intragroup comparisons affect perception, incentives, evaluation, and behavior?

    Stark, Oded, and J. Edward Taylor. 1989. “Relative Deprivation and International Migration.” Demography 26 (1): 1. doi:10.2307/2061490.
 

    I had research questions vaguely in mind, and I was already making mental comparisons between what I was seeing and what the sociology literature had to say.

    Duneier, Mitchell. 1999. Sidewalk. New York: Straus and Giroux.
 

    From the beginning of my time as a sociologist I’ve been interested in the struggle of human beings to live in accordance to moral worth. And one of the reasons that Sixth Avenue was a strategic site for me was that the challenges to living a moral life there were greater. Here was a setting where you had people coming out of prison with felony convictions and no ability to get jobs or housing. So the question was how you survive in those circumstances and still struggle to live in accordance to standards of moral worth?

    Duneier, Mitchell, and Les Back. 2006. “Voices from the Sidewalk: Ethnography and Writing Race.” Ethnic and Racial Studies 29 (3). Taylor & Francis Group: 543–65. doi:10.1080/01419870600598113.
 

Literature Review

Purposes

  • Foundation
  • Authority
  • Dialog

Questions

  • What is already known?
  • What concepts and theories are relevant?
  • What methods and strategies have been employed?
  • Are there significant controversies?
  • Are there inconsistencies?
  • Are there gaps?

Stories

  • Synthesized, inter-textual coherence
  • Progressive, inter-textual coherence
  • Non-coherence
  • Incomplete literature
  • Incommensurate literature
